Bee's Endagerment

Bees are increasingly endangered due to a combination of factors including habitat loss, pesticide use, climate change, and disease. As natural areas are cleared for agriculture and development, bees lose vital sources of food and nesting sites. Pesticides, particularly neonicotinoids, can harm bees' ability to navigate and reproduce.

How To Help

Planting bee-friendly flowers, avoiding chemical pesticides, supporting local beekeepers, and creating green spaces can all provide bees with the food and shelter they need. By taking small steps in our gardens and communities, we can all play a part in protecting these vital pollinators.

Benefits of The Bees

Bees are incredibly beneficial to the environment and to humans. They play a vital role in pollinating flowers, fruits, and vegetables, helping plants grow and produce food. About one-third of the food we eat depends on pollination, much of it by bees. They also support biodiversity by helping plants reproduce, which in turn supports other wildlife. Without bees, ecosystems would struggle, and food supplies could decline.
